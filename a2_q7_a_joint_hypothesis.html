given the model y = x_1^T \beta_1 + x_2^T \beta_2 + e , where both regressors are q \times 1 , how can we test the following ?

H_0 : \beta_1 = \beta_2 v H_1 : \beta_1 \ne \beta_2

in the 3rd edition of Stock & Watson on pages 230 to 231 there is an approach for the q=1 case which goes as follows :

y = b_0 + b_1 x_1 + b_2 x_2 + u

y = b_0 + b_1 x_1 + b_2 x_2 + \underbrace{b_2 x_1 - b_2 x_1}_{0} + u

y = b_0 + b_1 x_1 - b_2 x_1 + b_2 x_2 + b_2 x_1 + u

if both regressors are q \times 1 then the coefficients are both q \times 1

y = b_0 + \underbrace{(b_1 - b_2)}_{\gamma} x_1 + b_2 \underbrace{(x_1 + x_2)}_{w} + u

y = b_0 + \gamma \cdot x_1 + b_2 \cdot w + u

in the q=1 case, if we regress y ~ x1 + w , we can use the t-stat on \gamma
